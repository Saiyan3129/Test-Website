HouseEstablished 2014

A small workshop, twelve years.

Tester began as a single pair of loafers, made on a borrowed last in a Manila garage. It is now a small house with a Northampton workshop and a Manila showroom — but the way we work has not really changed.

In 2014, our founder Gerard cut a single pair of penny loafers from a piece of waxed Italian calfskin and gave them to a friend. They asked if he could make another. He could; he did; and within a year there was a workshop with three lasts, two stitchers, and a kettle.

For the first five years we made only loafers, only in three colours, and we wrote them up by hand on cards that our customers still have. We learned the difference between a shoe that lasts a season and a shoe that lasts twenty.

A shoe should be the dullest piece of clothing in your wardrobe — and the one you replace last.

In 2019 we took on a partial lease in a Northampton building — three rooms, a sole-press, and a gas heater that hasn't worked in two winters. The building was made for shoemaking; it knows what to do.

We are still small. We will not get larger than we can hand-finish. Every pair we make is signed by its maker on the inside of the heel-cup — the only place it shouldn't show.

N° 02Twelve Years

A short chronology.

  1. 2014 — Manila

    First pair, on a borrowed last

    Cut from a single piece of waxed Italian calfskin, in a garage with no proper bench.

  2. 2017 — Manila

    First showroom, Salcedo Village

    Three lasts, two stitchers, a kettle, a sign painted by a friend.

  3. 2019 — Northampton

    The Northampton workshop opens

    A 1924 brick building, formerly Patten & Sons, lasted by the same hands for forty years.

  4. 2022 — Plus Court

    Sneakers, finally

    The Plus Court releases — eight years of refusing, then a pattern that finally felt right.

  5. 2026 — House Mark

    The Atelier programme

    A loyalty programme that is mostly an excuse to invite people we like into the workshop, once a year.

N° 03The Factory

Inside the Northampton workshop.

Three rooms. The cutting room (windowed, north-facing), the lasting room (older floorboards), and the finishing room (where the photos are usually taken).

Eleven people work here, including two apprentices who will be makers in eighteen months. Visits are by appointment — see Showrooms.
